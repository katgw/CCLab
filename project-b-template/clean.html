CLEAN THE OCEAN!

What Happened?

IN OUR WORLD

Ocean pollution is a global crisis, with an estimated 8 million tons of plastic entering the oceans annually. It poses a severe threat to marine life, ecosystems, and human health. The accumulation of pollutants, including plastic debris, chemicals, and heavy metals, degrades water quality, disrupts marine habitats, and contributes to the decline of fisheries worldwide.

IN AUS

Australia's marine environments, such as the Great Barrier Reef (GBR), have borne the brunt of ocean pollution. The Great Barrier Reef is of the world's largest coral reef systems and faces ongoing threats from marine litter. An estimated 13,000 pieces of plastic litter are found per square kilometer in the GBR, threatening its delicate ecosystems.

IMPACT OF POLLUTION

Ocean pollution has far-reaching consequences, causing short term harm and potential long term endangerment to marine species. Plastic pollution can also be ingested by marine life or entangle and suffocate them. Similarly, coral reefs suffer from pollution-induced stress, which leads to coral bleaching and thus a decline in the diversity and habitats fostering marine life.

How Can I Help?

Over the years, many conservation initiatives have been implemented to mitigate the amount of pollution

that enters our marine ecosystems. Despite these efforts, marine biodiversity still remains vulnerable to ongoing pollutants.

SUSTAINABLE ENERGY

Advocate for and transition towards sustainable energy sources, like solar, wind, and hydroelectric. Reducing dependence on fossil fuels decreases the demand for oil extraction and transportation, subsequently lowering the likelihood of oil spills.

COASTAL CLEANUP EFFORTS

Engage in local community initiatives focused on coastal cleanup and restoration. Participate in efforts to remove oil and debris from shorelines to help mitigate the immediate impact of spills and protects vulnerable ecosystems.

SUPPORT NGOs

Volunteer with organizations that specialize in wildlife rehabilitation. These centers play a crucial role in rescuing and treating animals affected by oil spills, working towards their recovery and eventual release back into the wild.
