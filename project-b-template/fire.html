BUSHFIRES

What Happened?

IN OUR WORLD

Rising temperatures, prolonged droughts, and changing precipitation patterns have contributed to a surge in the frequency and intensity of wildfires worldwide. According to the World Meteorological Organization, the annual number of weather-related disasters, including wildfires, has nearly tripled since the 1960s. Increasing carbon emissions and global warming have played a huge role in the increase of this trend globally.

IN AUS

Australia has a long history with bushfires but climate change has exacerbated the situation within recent decades. In 2019-2020, Australia experienced a series of devastating fires, a period known as "Black Summer." During this time, an estimated 18 million hectares of land were burned , resulting in the destruction of ecosystems, the loss of many animals like koalas, and devastation to local communities.

IMPACT OF WILDFIRES

As climate change amplifies fire conditions, we can anticipate longer fire seasons and more extreme fire behaviour. These events lead to loss of human lives, destruction of homes, and severe ecological damage that cause species to become extinct or endangered. During Black Summer 3 billion animals died, emphasizing the urgent need for global climate action to address the escalating risks of wildfires.

How Can I Help?

Over the years, many conservation initiatives have been implemented to mitigate the amount of pollution

that enters our marine ecosystems. Despite these efforts, marine biodiversity still remains vulnerable to ongoing pollutants.

REDUCE CARBON FOOTPRINT

Minimize your carbon footprint by using energy-efficient appliances, taking public transport, and reducing overall energy consumption. Conserving energy lowers your impact on climate change and decreases the carbon emissions that contribute to more intense and frequent bushfires.

CONSERVE WATER

Practice water conservation at home by fixing leaks, using water-efficient appliances, and avoiding unnecessary water usage. Drought conditions often contribute to the spread of wildfires, and conserving water helps maintain environmental balance and reduce stress on ecosystems.

SUSTAINABLE AGRICULTURE

Choose sustainably sourced and produced food products. Agriculture practices, such as deforestation and large-scale monoculture, can contribute to environmental degradation and increased fire risks. Supporting regenerative and sustainable farming practices promotes healthier ecosystems and reduces the likelihood of wildfires.
